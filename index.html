Building Heat Pipes

My team developed a method for building heat pipes in classroom laboratory setting. We documented our findings for Transport Phenomena, a fluids course at Olin College.

Kinetic Sculpture

I was the project manager for a team that built a kinetic sculpture from waste materials. As people walked by, our sculpture would autonomously rise to greet them.

Failure Analysis

In a Failure Analysis class, I conducted an investigation on a corroding water bottle, utilizing SEM, EDS, and XRF trechnology to get to the bottom of the corrosion pits.

Peristaltic Pump Model


For this project in my Mechanical Design course, we chose machines we were interested in to disect and further learn from. I designed a simplified peristaltic pump in SolidWorks, then ran SolidWorks Motion and FEA to understand the load conditions.

Self-righting robot

In an Engineering Systems Analysis class, my team and I developed a PI control loop so that "Rocky" the robot could stand upright. We developed the control loop in Simulink, wrote the program in Matlab, and tuned the parameters to ensure Rocky would self-right with ease.

Repairability Rubric for Bose

For my senior capstone project at Olin College, I worked with fellow Olin seniors to improve the circularity of Bose products. We worked with Bose engineers for a year and developed a rubric to measure the repairability of their wearable products. The rubric has been integrated into the Bose Product Development requirements.

FRC - FEA on Double Jointed Arm

I preformed finite element analysis on model of a carbon fiber double jointed arm, comparing cylindrical and square tubing, and then found maximum loads possible at desired natural frequency.

FRC - Series Elastic Actuated Intake


A teammate and I designed, manufactured and built two intake arms which utilized a total of 17 sensors to semi-automatically actuate and take hold of milk crates.
